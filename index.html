I am a PhD student currently based at Queen's University, Belfast supervised by Dr. Alison Cameron, Dr. Nicki Whitehouse and Prof. Paulo Prodhol.

Background

My research is centred on environmental niche modelling and ancestral niche reconstruction. The ultimate goal of this work is to determine the climate preferences of species millions of years into the past. I use fossil beetles for my research; not only are they particulrly abundant in modern times, but they also have a pretty good fossil record.

After reading Natural Sciences at the University of Cambridge, I completed a masters in Management at Queen’s University in Belfast. Before commencing my PhD I worked as a software developer in the private sector doing some data warehousing and analytics. I'm still interested in data visualisation, data management and databases. I don’t have as much use for my data warehousing skills these days, but I regularly use R, Python and T-SQL for my PhD research. There's more detail in academic CV (which is likely to change as I keep renaming my thesis chapters!).

My research interests include niche modelling, palaeoecology, Quaternary science, palaeoclimate reconstructions, GIS, phylogenetics process automation and data science.
