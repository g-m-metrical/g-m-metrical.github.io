I am a PhD student currently based at Queen's University, Belfast supervised by Dr. Alison Cameron, Dr. Nicki Whitehouse and Prof. Paulo Prodöhl.

Background

My research is focused on environmental niche modelling (ENM) and ancestral niche reconstruction. The ultimate goal of this work is to develop methodologies to investigate the ancestral climate preferences, niche traits and biogeography of modern beetle taxa.

After reading Natural Sciences at the University of Cambridge, I completed a masters in Management at Queen’s University in Belfast. Before commencing my PhD I worked as a software developer in the private sector doing some data warehousing and data analytics. I'm still interested in data visualisation, data management and databases. Along with data warehousing software, I regularly use R, Python and T-SQL for my PhD research. There's more detail in academic CV (which is likely to change as I keep renaming my thesis chapters!).

My research interests include niche modelling, palaeoecology, Quaternary science, palaeoclimate reconstructions, GIS, phylogenetics, database development, process automation and data science.
